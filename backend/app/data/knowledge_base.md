# VaakSiddhi — App Knowledge

## What VaakSiddhi is
VaakSiddhi is a speech practice web app for children working with communication professionals (speech-language pathologists, therapists). It helps kids practice pronouncing words through a guided, game-like flow with animated character companions.

## How the app works
1. **Language Select** — child picks English, Hindi, or Kannada.
2. **Character Select** — child picks a companion: BOLT (brave space robot), ZARA (cheerful alien from planet Zorb), NOVA (calm, wise space AI), BEEP (tiny helper robot), ECHO (ancient computer from a distant galaxy), MIRA (friendly underwater robot). Each has a distinct voice and personality.
3. **Therapist Input** — a therapist or caregiver enters or selects the target word for the child to practice.
4. **Practice Screen** — the child listens to the character say the word, then records themselves saying it.
5. **Evaluation** — the recording is transcribed and scored for pronunciation accuracy, acoustic quality (loudness, pitch, speaking rate, voice quality), and specific phoneme-level accuracy (including tricky dental/retroflex and aspiration distinctions in Hindi and Kannada).
6. **Result Screen** — the child gets encouraging feedback from their character, a score, and tips.
7. **Drill Mode** — if a child struggles repeatedly with certain sounds, the app automatically builds a focused practice sequence targeting those specific phonemes.

## Features
- Multi-language support: English, Hindi, Kannada.
- Character voices with distinct personalities and audio effects.
- Acoustic feedback: loudness, pitch stability, speaking rate, voice quality (jitter/shimmer/HNR).
- Phoneme-level accuracy scoring, including commonly confused sound pairs (e.g. dental vs retroflex consonants in Hindi/Kannada).
- Encouragement system that adapts messages based on attempt number and condition.
- Drill mode for targeted repeated practice on struggling sounds.
- Designed with autism-friendly and communication-difference-friendly considerations in mind (condition-aware feedback tone).

## Who it's for
Children working with speech-language pathologists or therapists, and the professionals/caregivers supporting them.

# General Speech Therapy Background

## What articulation/pronunciation therapy involves
Speech therapy for pronunciation typically focuses on helping a child produce specific sounds (phonemes) correctly, in isolation, then in words, then in sentences, then in conversation. Progress is usually gradual and attempt-by-attempt improvement matters more than getting it perfect immediately.

## Common encouragement principles
- Celebrate effort and attempts, not just correct outcomes.
- Keep feedback specific and actionable ("try opening your mouth wider" rather than just "wrong").
- Repetition in a low-pressure, game-like context helps children stay engaged longer.
- Short, frequent practice sessions are often more effective than long infrequent ones.

## Common sound difficulties
- Substitution: replacing one sound with another (e.g. "wabbit" for "rabbit").
- Distortion: sound is attempted but comes out unclear.
- Omission: sound is dropped entirely.
- In Hindi and Kannada specifically, dental vs retroflex consonant pairs (त/ट, थ/ठ, द/ड, etc.) and aspirated vs unaspirated pairs (क/ख, ग/घ, etc.) are common areas of difficulty, since these distinctions don't exist in English and require precise tongue placement.

## Working with children with autism or communication differences
- Predictable structure and routine helps reduce anxiety.
- Visual and auditory consistency (same character voice, same flow) builds comfort.
- Avoid overwhelming with correction — pick one thing to focus on at a time.
- Sensory considerations matter: some children may be sensitive to loud or sudden sounds, so feedback tone matters.

## When to consult a professional
This app is a practice tool, not a diagnostic tool. Concerns about a child's speech development, hearing, or broader communication should always be directed to a licensed speech-language pathologist or pediatrician.
